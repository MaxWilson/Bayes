Boring Explanation

Bayes' Theorem says that P(H | Evidence) = P(Evidence | H) * P(H) / P(Evidence), where P(Evidence) is a normalization term that makes probabilities still sum to one.

Before the election, Nate Silver computed that there was a 71% chance of Hillary winning. If H is the hypothesis that "538 is better than a coin flip for predicting election results", and Evidence is "Trump wins the election", then P(Evidence | H) = 0.29, P(Evidence) = P(Evidence | H) * P(H) + P(Evidence | not H) * P(not H), and P(H | Evidence) = 0.29 * P(H) / P(Evidence).
